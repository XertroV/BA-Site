Bitcoin Australia.

We are Australia's not-for-profit association dedicated to the standardisation, protection, and promotion of Bitcoin and cryptocurrency in the region.

Mission Join Us

Our Mission

Australia is at the forefront of the digital currency revolution.

Bitcoin represents a unique opportunity - both technologically and economically - previously unavailable to the world.

Education

We seek to reduce knowledge barriers to entry, thereby growing the Bitcoin ecosystem.

For individual and institutional users we provide objective, fair and balanced practical information, consultation and advice.

Standardisation

We seek to protect the local ecosystem by bringing a necessary level of conformity.

To that end we create content, standards and guidelines of best practice for interacting with the technology.

Safe Bitcoin Practices

Protection

We seek to protect Bitcoin, as the most promising open-source project and p2p network on the planet.

Bitcoin is secure when this source of strength is maintained. We achieve this by fostering those creating services on the open architecture.

About The Board

Our executives are entrenched in the Bitcoin community regionally, running the full gamut of expertise.

Martin Bajalan | Board member

Martin has background in Computing and Mathematics and has managed development of several enterprise level software products in the area of e-commerce, credit card payment gateways, trading and ERP systems over the past 14 years.
After hearing about Bitcoin in late 2012, Martin took leave from the research program in order to develop a trading platform for digital currencies which was launched in September 2013. Martin actively supports industry regulation for Bitcoin and also cooperation with governments in the areas of AML, and Fraud prevention.

Max Kaye | Treasurer

As a cryptocurrency enthusiast, and a member of the Bitcoin community since 2010, Max has a vast array of experience dealing with *coin based technology; from running a NMC/BTC mining pool in 2011, to experimenting with trustless exchange in 2013.

Adam Poulton | Secretary

Adam is a self employed business owner and father of two living in Tasmania. Adam has a Degree in Applied Science from the University of Tasmania and has also had an interest in money and macro economics from an early age.
Since the global financial crisis in 2008, Adam has been studying economics, in particular the theories of the Austrian School. This has lead to his interest in Bitcoin, and his belief that this new currency can solve many of the worlds current economic problems. Adam is currently in Beta in a Bitcoin startup, designed to integrate Bitcoin into peoples daily lives, and hopes that in the future, his children will be more familiar with Bitcoin than with regular dollars.

Bitcoin FAQ's.

Bitcoins are digital coins you can send through the internet, person to person without going through a financial institution

Bitcoin Currency

Bitcoin was 1st outlined in a white paper by Satoshi Nakomoto in 2008.

Bitcoin was released on Jan 3 2009. Bitcoin is a currency and a payment system rolled into one.

Bitcoin

All Bitcoin transactions are recorded in the "Blockchain" that can be inspected by anyone online, Blockchain.info

A Bitcoin is divisible to 8 decimal places, the units are called 'Satoshis'. The decentralised network of computers that run the Bitcoin software, belong to no bank, government or corporation. These computers perpetuate and audit the Bitcoin network.

Finding Bitcoins?

Bitcoins can be sent, person to person across the internet, instantaneously, with minimal or no fees. Bitcoins are traded globally, on international and local currency exchanges, and in person. They can be bought and sold for all major currencies.
